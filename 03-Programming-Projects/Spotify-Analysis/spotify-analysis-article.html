Spotify Popularity Analysis (Part 1): Exploring Genres, Artists, Keys, and Tracks

Introduction

This study explores track popularity across genres, artists, keys, and songs using the Spotify Features dataset. By combining descriptive statistics with visualizations, we highlight trends in listening behavior, identify dominant categories, and examine whether musical attributes influence popularity.

Genre Analysis

Average Popularity by Genre — Genres like pop, rap, rock, and hip-hop rank highest. Niche categories like opera or anime trend much lower, reflecting mainstream bias on Spotify.

Distribution of Popularity — Boxplots show wide spreads within pop and rap, meaning mega-hits and flops coexist in the same genre. Folk-like genres show tighter, lower distributions.

Frequency & Proportion — Pie and bar charts confirm Spotify's catalog is heavily skewed toward a handful of genres, though long-tail diversity persists.

Artist Analysis

Average Popularity — Artists such as Pedro Capó, Mario Bautista, Mau y Ricky, Martin Garrix, and Billie Eilish lead by average popularity. Many are Latin or reggaeton, showing global streaming trends.

Distributions — Top artists often release many tracks, but not all are hits. Boxplots reveal broad distributions compared to the global average.

Frequency — Prolific output ≠ higher average popularity. Some artists dominate catalog size but not listener appeal.

Musical Key Analysis

Average Popularity by Key — Keys like C#, B, F#, and G# show slightly higher averages, but differences are minimal. Musical key is not a strong predictor of popularity compared to genre or artist.

Distributions - Distribution of Popularity — The spread of popularity scores across keys is fairly uniform, with no key showing a distinctly wider or tighter distribution; this supports the finding that key choice has little impact on listener appeal.

Frequency — Keys such as C, G, and D dominate because they're easier for composition, not necessarily more popular with listeners.

Track Analysis

Top Songs — Chart-toppers like 7 Rings, Wow., Con Calma, Sunflower, and Thank U, Next score near 100 popularity. Popularity is extremely concentrated: a handful of tracks dominate streams.

Comparative Distribution Analyses

  • Genres: Top 10 genres outperform catalog average.
  • Artists: Top 10 by average popularity outperform; top 10 by volume may not.
  • Keys: Show minimal difference from overall distribution.

Conclusions

Genre and artist are the strongest predictors of popularity. Keys have little effect. At the track level, success is heavily concentrated in a small set of global hits.

Caveat: The dataset is skewed toward popular tracks, so results may not reflect the full Spotify catalog.
