Algorithmic Expressions: Exploring how randomness and rules create cohesion

Introduction

Algorithmic Expressions is a set of generative art programs I developed in Processing. Each piece demonstrates how code, randomness, recursion, and geometry can come together to produce complex and unique visual patterns.

The collection so far includes five distinct models: Displacement, Gyration, MazeTiler, PathFinder, and Recursion. While each follows its own ruleset, they all share the goal of blending structured systems with controlled randomness to mimic qualities of organic and abstract art.

Displacement

Displacement fills the canvas with rectangles whose size, color, opacity, and position are slightly randomized. Additional texture is added by layering noise-based circles within each rectangle, giving the result a grainy, tactile quality.

  • Key Idea: Offset grids create tension between order and randomness.
  • Visual Effect: A quilt-like mosaic where colors and textures blend unpredictably.

Gyration

Gyration uses rotational symmetry to place shapes (ellipses, squares, triangles) in a spiraling pattern around the canvas. Each cycle grows in size and distance, creating a hypnotic vortex of geometry.

  • Key Idea: Iterative shape placement with adjustable increments.
  • Visual Effect: Expanding concentric rings of forms, sometimes appearing kaleidoscopic.

MazeTiler

MazeTiler divides the canvas into tiles. Within each tile, random diamonds and diagonal lines are drawn, combining to form intricate, maze-like lattices. Each run generates a different maze pattern.

  • Key Idea: Local randomness scaled across a tiled grid.
  • Visual Effect: Geometric tapestries resembling woven fabric or puzzles.

PathFinder

PathFinder simulates wandering lines that avoid previously visited coordinates. Each line makes directional decisions step-by-step, producing branching, labyrinthine pathways.

  • Key Idea: Constraint-driven movement across the canvas.
  • Visual Effect: Abstract cartographic maps, where lines weave through space without overlap.

Recursion

Recursion builds tiles that recursively generate smaller tiles within themselves. Shapes (rectangles, ellipses, triangles) are placed in quadrants, but tiles can also subdivide, leading to deeply nested structures.

  • Key Idea: Recursive subdivision drives complexity.
  • Visual Effect: Fractal-like mosaics that balance structure and unpredictability.

Conclusion

Together, these five models form a cohesive exploration of generative art techniques. From grids and spirals to mazes and recursion, each project demonstrates how algorithmic systems can produce artwork that feels organic, surprising, and alive.

While each sketch can stand on its own, their shared use of randomness, geometry, and rules highlights the endless creative potential of procedural design.
